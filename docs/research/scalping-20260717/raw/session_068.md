# session_068: Intraday Momentum into Close — Replication Status

**Date**: 2026-07-17  
**Question**: Market intraday momentum into the close (first half-hour predicts last half-hour): replication status

---

## Replication verdict

Evidence is mixed historically but negative for current U.S. trading. The anomaly replicated within 1996–2013 U.S. data, yet failed in Australia, several APAC markets, a peer-reviewed post-sample study, and 2021–2026 U.S. data. Treat it as a regime-dependent historical regularity—not a currently validated retail edge.

- **Gao–Han–Li–Zhou close momentum — Grade A.** Market: SPY, 30-minute intervals. Signal \(r_1\) is previous 16:00 close→10:00 return, thus includes the overnight gap. At 15:30, long if \(r_1>0\), otherwise short; exit 16:00 closing print; no stop. Exclude days with fewer than 500 trades. Over February 1993–December 2013 (roughly 5,200 sessions), reported annual return 6.67%, volatility 6.19%, Sharpe 1.08, win rate 54.37%, \(R^2=1.6\%\), recursive OOS \(R^2=1.4\%\); PF unreported. After quoted-spread costs, 2001–2013 return was 4.46%; commissions and additional slippage were omitted. A dual-confirmation variant trades only when \(r_1\) and 15:00–15:30 return share a sign: 4.39% annual return, Sharpe 0.98. Its advertised 77.05% "success" misleadingly counts no-trade days; active-only return/Sharpe were 8.85%/1.39. [Paper and rules](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X18301351). Pine v6: **yes**, using 1–15-minute bars and New York sessions.

- **Independent U.S./APAC replication — Grade A.** Identical return definitions; statistical regression rather than a separately costed strategy. U.S. 1996–2013, N=4,094: \(R^2=1.7\%\), OOS \(R^2=1.7\%\)—successful historical replication. China N=2,049 and Japan N=3,804 were positive; Hong Kong N=4,959 and Singapore N=3,261 were null/negative; South Korea N=2,944 was marginal. Australia's separate N=3,850 replication found no significant STW effect. No win rate, PF, stops, or trading costs were reported. [APAC study](https://researchmgt.monash.edu/ws/files/519509174/494419119_oa.pdf), [Australia](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0927538X21000068). Pine: **yes** mechanically, but economic implementability is unproven.

- **Modern exact replication — Grade B.** SPY/QQQ/IWM, same previous-close→10:00 signal; enter 15:30 in its direction, exit 16:00, no stop. January 2021–July 2026, N=1,370: SPY \(t=-2.3\), gross −0.55 bp/day; IWM −26.6% cumulative. Costs, win rate and PF unreported. A peer-reviewed futures study likewise found the anomaly disappearing OOS. [Current methodology](https://www.vortexcapitalgroup.com/insights/the-1pm-echo-treasury-auctions-are-the-last-living-signal-on-the-half-hour-clock), [peer-reviewed OOS result](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/fut.22375). Pine: **yes**. Retail-cost survival: **no**—gross expectancy is already negative.

- **Treasury-auction echo — Grade B; promising alternative, not a replication.** On scheduled coupon-auction days, read TLT's 13:00–13:30 return; trade SPY/QQQ/IWM in that direction at 13:30; exit 14:00, or 13:55 on FOMC days; no stop. N=501: hit rates 55.3%/55.1%/54.1%, gross +2.1/+2.8/+3.3 bp. Long-end auctions N=83 reached IWM 61.4%, +5.5 bp. No PF or untouched OOS; costs excluded. Pine: **partial**—TLT is accessible, but the auction calendar requires external/manual data. SPY's 2.1-bp edge is fragile after spread, slippage and bar-close fill error.

- **Noise-area/VWAP momentum — Grade B; adjacent strategy.** SPY, decisions every 30 minutes: long/short when price breaches gap-adjusted bands based on the prior 14 days' same-time absolute move from the open. Exit on VWAP/current-band crossover or 16:00; volatility-targeted sizing capped at 4×. May 2007–April 2024: 7,668 trades, 37% trade win rate, 1,985% total, 19.6% CAGR, Sharpe 1.33, MDD 25%; PF unreported. Costs assume $0.0035 commission plus only $0.001 slippage/share. No untouched OOS; stops, sizing and parameters were refined on the same sample. [Rules and results](https://concretumgroup.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Beat-the-Market.pdf). Pine: **yes/partial**; logic is feasible, but exact IQFeed fills and leverage are not.

Survivorship bias is limited for SPY itself, but ex-post selection of liquid surviving ETFs remains. The larger risks are publication decay, regime dependence, multiple testing and same-sample rule refinement. Overall: **do not deploy first-half-hour→close momentum without a fresh broker-specific, post-2020 tick/quote replication; present evidence says the standalone edge has decayed.**
