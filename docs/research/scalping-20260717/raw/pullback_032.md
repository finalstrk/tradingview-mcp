# Research: Keltner/Bollinger Band Tag Pullback with Higher-Timeframe Trend Filter

**topic_id**: pullback_032  
**family**: pullback  
**date**: 2026-07-17  
**question**: Keltner/Bollinger band tag pullback with higher-timeframe trend filter: evidence

---

The direct evidence is weak. I found no peer-reviewed or reproducible code+data study that validates the full combination—1–15-minute band-tag pullback plus higher-timeframe trend filter—after realistic costs and out-of-sample testing.

- **USA500 BB extreme pullback with 30-minute trend.** USA500 CFD/index, 1-minute bars, November 2022–November 2025. Long when both 1m and 30m EMA(50)>EMA(100), close<lower BB(20,3σ), and RSI(14)<25; shorts inverse. Enter at close; initial and trailing stop 2×ATR(14); 10-bar cooldown; no session filter. Reported: 162 trades, 40.74% wins, PF 1.30, Sharpe 1.00, +128.0%, −18.65% max drawdown versus +80.0% buy-and-hold. No commission, spread, slippage, or OOS; BID-only data and 50× leverage. Average trade was only 0.0132%—about 1.3 bp—so a retail CFD spread likely destroys the edge. Data are not supplied. **Grade B. Pine v6: yes**, using confirmed 30m `request.security()` values. Strong selection/parameter-overfit risk; HTF lookahead must be prevented. [Notebook and results](https://gist.github.com/kumrzz/de68d06ffc935fe7e1166fe882e1f0c5)

- **Keltner centreline pullback.** S&P 500 stocks; timeframe and universe construction undisclosed. After an upper-channel breakout, buy a pullback within 0.5% of the 20-EMA centreline when that EMA slopes upward and RSI>40; stop below lower KC; target upper KC, advertised minimum 2:1. Shorts inverse. Claimed 3,000 signals over 12 years: 58% long/60% short wins, PF 2.26, average win 3.1%, loss 1.5%. No trades, code, data, costs, OOS, session rules, or actual HTF filter are provided; cited "studies" are not identifiable. These are marketing claims, not usable evidence. **Grade C. Pine v6: yes**, but intraday profitability is unsubstantiated. Survivorship and universe-selection risk are severe; cost survival cannot be assessed. [Source](https://www.tradealgo.com/trading-guides/technical-analysis/keltner-channels-volatility-based-trading-bands-for-trend-and-breakout-traders)

- **Short-Term Bollinger Reversion 2.0.** USD/CAD, 1-hour. Exponential BB(50,2), RSI(9). Long after the low tags the lower band, close returns inside, and RSI crosses above 25 while below 50; next-open entry. Short inverse at 75. Stop 50 pips beyond signal high/low; half exit after middle-band touch, remainder after opposite-band touch; move stop to breakeven. No HTF/session filter. August 2017–early January 2018: 75% wins, +397.5 pips/+7.95%; trade count and PF undisclosed, one trade supplied 105 pips. No costs or OOS. **Grade B. Pine v6: yes**, including 1–15m mechanically, but no evidence supports that transfer. Hand-testing and deliberately choosing "ranging" USD/CAD create selection/overfit risk; spread survival is unknown. [Rules/results](https://www.babypips.com/trading/system-short-term-bollinger-reversion-backtest-2)

- **Stationary Bollinger breakout—important negative control.** CSI300 index futures, 15/30/60-second data, 2012–2016: 1.28m/638k/319k observations. Enter when normalized Bollinger position crosses ±K; exit on recross; flatten daily. Among 72 variants, the best gross 30-second rule had 44,559 long/short deals, 50.79% wins, annual return 59.31%, Sharpe 2.33, drawdown 6.64%, average-profit/loss 1.62. After actual exchange costs, **every strategy lost money and none remained statistically significant**; SPA testing addressed data snooping. It is breakout rather than HTF-filtered pullback, but directly demonstrates how high-frequency band edges vanish after costs. **Grade A** (Physica A paper). **Pine v6: partial**—logic yes, sub-minute replication/data fidelity no. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.07470)

Overall: the only direct HTF-filtered intraday result is a zero-cost, single-market notebook with a 1.3-bp average trade. The strongest evidence is negative. A retail-tradable edge should be presumed absent until walk-forward tests include spread, commission, slippage, confirmed HTF bars, multiple markets, and an untouched final OOS period.
