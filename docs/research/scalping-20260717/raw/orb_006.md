# Day-Type Classification (Trend vs Range) as an ORB Filter

**topic_id:** orb_006  
**family:** orb  
**date:** 2026-07-17  
**question:** Day-type classification (trend day vs range day) as an ORB filter: methods and measured impact

---

The strongest conclusion is negative: "trend day" is usually identifiable only after the profitable move has occurred. Tradeable proxies—opening volatility, relative volume, gaps, and OR width—show mixed and regime-dependent results.

1. **Volatility-state ORB.** Crude-oil and S&P 500 futures, 1991–2011, daily/intraday thresholds. Place long and short stops at open ±ρ (tested 0.5–2%); first trigger establishes direction, opposite threshold limits loss; exit at close. Ex-post volatility deciles showed highest-minus-lowest-state ORB returns of about 200 bp/day for crude and 150 bp/day for S&P; assumed round-trip costs were 8 bp and 6 bp respectively, plus a 2-bp entry-jump test. Roughly 5,000 sessions per market; no win rate/PF reported. Crucial flaw: the "state" uses that day's absolute open-to-close return—unavailable at entry. Their feasible OOS approach merely raises ρ; GARCH forecasts did not improve results. **Grade A** (refereed). Pine: **yes** for threshold strategy; **no** for the ex-post classifier. Edge may survive stated futures costs in extreme-volatility tails, but not as a known-at-open filter. [Paper](https://www.ifta.org/assets/docs/d_ifta_journal_19.pdf)

2. **Stocks-in-Play relative-volume filter.** All U.S. stocks, 5-minute bars, 2016–2023. Eligible: price >$5, prior-14-day ADV ≥1m, ATR14 >$0.50; first-five-minute volume / prior-14 opening-volume average ≥1; trade daily top 20. Bullish first candle → buy-stop at its high; bearish → sell-stop at its low; doji skipped. Stop 0.10×ATR from entry; otherwise exit 16:00 ET; risk 1%, leverage ≤4×. Base ORB: 29% total, 3.2% IRR, Sharpe 0.48, hit rate 41.4%. Filtered: 1,637%, 41.6% IRR, Sharpe 2.81, hit 48.4%, MDD 12%; $0.0035/share commission. Trade count and genuine OOS period were not reported. QuantConnect's executable 2016 replication produced Sharpe 2.396. **Grade A for replication / B for eight-year claim.** Pine: **partial**—single-symbol rules yes; point-in-time top-20 universe ranking no. Major overfitting, short-locate, delisting/survivorship, spread and opening-slippage risks; commission-only results are unlikely to transfer intact. [Paper](https://www.wealth-lab.com/api/discussion/download/pdf/8007-ssrn-4729284-1-pdf), [code/replication](https://www.quantconnect.com/research/18444/opening-range-breakout-for-stocks-in-play/)

3. **VVG day classifier.** MNQ, five-minute, 947 sessions (2021–2025). After 10:00 ET classify positive when absolute first-30-minute return, overnight gap, and first-bar volume deviation each exceed expanding-window 66.7th percentiles. The strongest test added an OLS reversal condition, entered next-bar open against the opening move, exited at close, and charged two MNQ points round-trip: 127 OOS trades, 52.0% wins, +7.80 points/trade, T=1.46; 2024 lost 26.75 points. Continuation was −3.22 points, 48.8% wins. **Grade B** (transparent preprint, no independently reproduced data/code). Pine: **partial**; expanding percentiles/OLS are possible but cumbersome. It fails statistical and year-stability gates, so it is not presently a credible ORB filter after retail friction. [Study](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11423)

4. **Opening-range-width classifier.** A 240,102-trade commercial study reports five-minute ORB win rates falling from 51.0% for width <$0.50 (45,582 trades) to 34.6% above $2 (13,960); confirmed breakout, opposite-side stop, standardized target/session-close exit. Yet a separate 6,142-session ES/NQ study finds *wide* 30-minute ORs (>0.6×ATR14) have 77.5%/74.2% directional continuation. **Grade B. Pine: yes.** The contradiction is definitional: target/stop profitability versus close-direction continuation, dollars versus ATR normalization. Neither reports realistic costs or OOS selection; raw continuation is not profit. [Width study](https://orbsetups.com/research/how-to-identify-and-avoid-false-breakouts-a-data-driven-approach/), [ES/NQ study](https://tradingstats.net/orb-breakout-strategy-guide/)

Overall: relative volume has the best reported impact, but lacks clean OOS/cost validation. Direct day-type classification evidence is weak or negative; no robust proof shows that a Pine-implementable trend/range gate reliably improves ORB net of realistic retail execution.
