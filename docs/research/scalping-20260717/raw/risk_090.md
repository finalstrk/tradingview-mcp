# Research Report: risk_090

**Topic ID:** risk_090  
**Family:** risk  
**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Question:** Break-even stop moves and trailing rules for scalps: quantified effects on expectancy

---

No candidate provides clean, cost-adjusted evidence that moving a scalp stop to break-even improves expectancy. The strongest evidence is mixed-to-negative; no study isolates break-even timing with credible out-of-sample testing.

1. Value-Area Breakout with time-aware exits — ES futures, one-second data. Entry: break previous-session value-area boundary, pull back into the former value area, then re-enter outside it in the breakout direction. Exit: tested 12 fixed stop distances versus a stack combining trailing stop, break-even tightening and no-progress timeout; indexed material does not disclose the stack's numerical thresholds. Filter: avoid the first 30 RTH minutes. January 2025–January 2026, 6,284 events. Unmanaged mean −2.20 ticks (t=−1.37, p=.17); excluding one anomalous month −0.19 ticks (p=.74). Every fixed price stop destroyed value; maximum stop selectivity was 69% versus approximately 75% required. The time-aware stack beat the price-stop baseline in 11/13 months (sign test p=.02), but absolute net expectancy, win rate, PF and costs are unreported. Grade B: detailed SSRN working paper, not peer-reviewed/reproduced. Pine: partial—bar-based value area and exits are possible, but 1–15-minute OHLC cannot reproduce one-second event ordering or precise volume-at-price construction. Single market, 13 months, exit-stack selection and regime dependence create serious overfitting risk; no survivorship issue in the equity sense. With the raw signal near zero before friction, retail costs probably erase it. [Source](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6350238)

2. Probability Trailing Stop — EUR/USD, USD/CAD and four Dow stocks, M1. Entry: random long/short direction. Initial TP and SL: 50 ticks each. Exit: after favorable movement, remaining distance to TP is multiplied by stop ratio; at ratio 1, if 30 ticks remain, stop trails 30 ticks behind current price, ratcheting only favorably. Q1 2009; no session filter. At least 300 trades per instrument and over 1,700 for EUR/USD. PF: EUR/USD .96, USD/CAD .88, DIS .91, MSFT 1.00, WMT .87, XOM .87. Win rates, costs and OOS absent. Grade B: concrete methodology, no code/data. Pine: yes, although intrabar assumptions require bar magnifier/lower-timeframe data. Cross-asset testing reduces survivorship concerns somewhat, but one quarter and one selected ratio invite temporal/parameter overfit. Because every gross PF is ≤1, spreads and commissions make expectancy unequivocally negative. [Source](https://www.investing.com/analysis/trading-with-probability-trailing-stops-145698)

3. PDT Strategic Trailing — current NIFTY-50 constituents, XAUUSD and EURUSD, 5-minute. Entry: long when a Permutation Decision Tree predicts an increase over the next five bars using swing levels, order-block flag, MA20/50 and RSI; no shorts. Exit: model sell prediction or ratcheting trailing stop; the published summary does not disclose the trail threshold, preventing exact replication. No special session filter beyond market data availability. Approximately 218,750 bars; chronological 80/20 split, 43,750 test bars. Test return 1.1802%; LSTM .557%, RNN .5896%, buy-and-hold −2.29%. Trade count, WR, PF, slippage and commissions are absent. Grade A only because it is peer-reviewed—not because the trading evidence is strong. Pine: partial; a fixed exported tree is feasible, but training PDT dynamically is impractical. Current-constituent selection risks survivorship bias; three months, many features and hyperparameters imply extreme overfitting risk. The small gross gain is unlikely to survive Indian-equity spread, taxes and commissions. [Paper](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0960077925013657), [preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.12828)

4. NQ break-even/ATR trail comparison — NQ, 5-minute cash session. Entry rule and sample size are undisclosed. Management: stop to break-even at +1R; compare fixed 20-tick trail, 1.5×ATR(14) trail and discretionary structure trail. October 2024–January 2025: respectively WR/PF 42%/1.1, 48%/1.6 and 51%/1.8; no costs or OOS. Grade C. Pine: yes for fixed/ATR, no for discretionary structure. Severe reporting, selection and overfit risk; PF 1.1 almost certainly dies after NQ friction, while PF 1.6 remains unsubstantiated. [Source](https://proptradingvibes.com/blog/trailing-stop-loss-strategy)

Bottom line: break-even is not "free protection"; it truncates trades whose normal MAE revisits entry. The defensible Pine experiment is an entry-frozen, walk-forward comparison of no-BE versus BE triggers at 0.5–2R, with tick-aware fills and full round-trip friction.
