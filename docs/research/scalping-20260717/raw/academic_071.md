# Market Intraday Momentum Research

**Topic ID:** academic_071  
**Family:** academic  
**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Question:** Gao-Han-Li-Zhou "Market Intraday Momentum": findings, replications, and current status

---

The central finding is statistically credible but economically fragile: first/last-half-hour dependence replicates across several markets, yet the original SPY rule failed a genuine post-sample code replication. It is intraday timing, not a high-frequency "scalp"; spreads can consume much of the small last-30-minute payoff.

1. **Gao–Han–Li–Zhou first-half-hour rule — Grade A.** Market/timeframe: SPY, 1-minute data aggregated to 30 minutes, 1993–2013, roughly 5,200 eligible sessions. Entry: at 15:30 ET, long if return from previous close to 10:00 is positive; otherwise short. Exit: 16:00 close; no stop/filter. Reported: 54.37% success, 6.67% annual return, 6.19% volatility, Sharpe 1.08; PF not reported. Recursive OOS \(R^2=1.2\%\). For 2005–2013, return falls from 7.96% gross to 6.52% using observed proportional spreads. The peer-reviewed paper also finds stronger results under high opening volatility/volume. [Original paper](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X18301351). Pine v6: **yes**, on 1–15-minute SPY bars, provided previous-close/session handling is exact.

However, a coded replication using SPY/IWM/IYR over 2015–2020 reports Sharpe **−0.628**, versus 0.582 for SPY; only the COVID crash subperiod worked (Sharpe 1.452). Win rate, PF and explicit cost results were not supplied. [QuantConnect replication and code](https://www.quantconnect.com/research/15348/intraday-etf-momentum/p1). This is strong negative post-sample evidence.

2. **Opening-plus-penultimate consensus — Grade A.** SPY/30-minute. At 15:30, go long only when both previous-close→10:00 and 15:00→15:30 returns are positive; short only when both are negative; otherwise abstain. Close 16:00; no stop. Same ~5,200-session sample: 77.05% success on traded days, 4.39% annual return, Sharpe 0.98; PF/trade count not reported; joint OOS \(R^2=1.8\%\). Post-2005 cost-adjusted return 4.74% versus 5.50% gross. [Paper PDF](https://jplinvest.dk/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Intraday-momentum-The-first-half-hour-return-predicts-the-last-half-hour-return.pdf). Pine: **yes**. Higher hit rate does not mean higher expectancy; abstention and smaller winners reduce return.

3. **Return-to-close futures momentum — Grade A.** Markets: 17 equity-index, 16 government-bond, 21 commodity and 8 currency futures; one-minute data, 1974–2020. Entry: 30 minutes before each market's close, long if return from previous close to entry is positive, otherwise short. Exit: close; no stop. Equal-weight asset-class portfolios produced: equities 6.86%/year, Sharpe 1.73, 55% success; bonds 2.16%, 1.62, 55%; commodities approximately Sharpe 1.29; currencies approximately 0.87–1.03. PF not reported. Expanding-window OOS tests were positive for the full-day signal; authors nevertheless omit costs, stating only that S&P futures remained positive at one-tick cost. [JFE paper](https://www3.nd.edu/~zda/intramom.pdf). Pine: **partial**—individual continuous futures yes; correctly rolled, equally weighted portfolios no. Retail survival is plausible mainly in ES and similarly liquid contracts, not broadly after spreads/roll/slippage.

4. **SPY "Noise Area" breakout — Grade B.** SPY/1-minute, 2007–April 2024. At each HH:00/HH:30 from 10:00 onward, compare price with bands based on the mean absolute open-to-same-time movement over the prior 14 sessions, gap-adjusted around the larger/smaller of today's open and yesterday's close. Break above/below enters long/short. Exit at close or when price crosses the current band/VWAP trailing stop; reversal allowed. Reported 7,668 trades: 43% hit rate, 380% total return, 9.7% annualized, Sharpe 1.24, 12% drawdown at fixed size; volatility-scaled version returned 1,985%, Sharpe 1.33, but used up to 4× leverage. PF and clean holdout were not reported. Costs assume $0.0035/share plus only $0.001/share slippage. [Detailed paper](https://alexandria.unisg.ch/server/api/core/bitstreams/a99aba00-f967-49b3-aceb-f544dc386e0b/content). Pine: **yes**, although same-time historical arrays require care. Retail viability is uncertain: the slippage assumption is exceptionally optimistic and the band, VWAP stop, volatility target and leverage refinements are all selected on the same sample.

Overall, survivorship bias is low for single-instrument SPY tests, but contract-roll bias affects futures and parameter/data-snooping risk is substantial. International evidence is mixed: 12/16 developed markets replicated statistically, whereas Australia, Hong Kong and Singapore did not. The defensible conclusion is that intraday return continuation exists conditionally—especially during volatile, liquid, stressed sessions—but the unfiltered GHLZ retail strategy is not currently established as profitable after realistic execution.
