# Research: Volatility-Breakout Systems (Intraday) Evidence

**topic_id**: squeeze_049  
**family**: squeeze  
**date**: 2026-07-17  
**question**: Volatility-breakout systems (Larry Williams style) compressed to intraday: evidence

---

Overall finding: evidence is mixed. Simple intraday volatility breakouts can show gross momentum, especially near the open, but the strongest retail-style results lack genuine out-of-sample validation or realistic execution costs. A recent MNQ falsification study finds the apparent edge generally too small to trade.

1. **Timely Opening Range Breakout (TORB)** — DJIA, S&P 500, Nasdaq-100, HSI and TAIEX futures; 1-minute bars. Define the high/low from the cash-market open through a market-specific probe time; enter on the first subsequent price crossing that boundary; close at the underlying-market close. Best probe times were 4/1/1 minutes for DJIA/ES/NQ, 151 minutes HSI and 37 minutes TAIEX. No stop or target. Full samples contained 1,381–3,099 trades over 2001/03–2011/13. Net annual returns after an assumed 0.01% per-trade cost: 8.95% ES, 12.89% DJIA, 17.51% NQ, 10.32% HSI, 20.28% TAIEX; reported p-values below 3%. Win rate/PF not reported. Subperiod results were mostly positive, but probe times were selected using the full sample—not true OOS. **Grade A**: peer-reviewed IEEE Access. **Pine v6: yes**, using session ranges and stop entries. Futures avoid equity survivorship bias, but probe-time selection across many alternatives creates data-mining risk. The 1-bp cost is optimistic once slippage and commissions are included; modern retail survival is plausible only for the larger reported returns, and requires fresh OOS replication. [Paper and rules](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/331076454_Assessing_the_Profitability_of_Timely_Opening_Range_Breakout_on_Index_Futures_Markets), [journal record](https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2019.2899177).

2. **5-minute ORB on "Stocks in Play"** — roughly 7,000 NYSE/Nasdaq stocks, 2016–2023. Require price >$5, 14-day average volume ≥1m shares, ATR(14) >$0.50, opening relative volume ≥100%; trade only the top 20 relative-volume stocks. If the 09:30–09:35 candle is bullish, buy its high; if bearish, short its low; doji means no trade. Stop is 10% of daily ATR; otherwise exit 16:00 ET. Reported net return 1,637%, IRR 41.6%, hit rate 48.4%, Sharpe 2.81, MDD 12%; trade count and PF not reported. Commission is $0.0035/share, but spread, slippage, borrow availability and market impact are omitted. CRSP includes delisted stocks, substantially reducing survivorship bias. No OOS: filters were developed and evaluated on the same period. **Grade B**: detailed SSRN methodology, not peer reviewed. **Pine: partial**—single-symbol rule yes; Pine cannot dynamically rank the entire US market's top-20 relative volume. The large margin may survive modest friction, but volatile news stocks and short execution make the published result unproven. [Paper](https://www.alexandria.unisg.ch/server/api/core/bitstreams/3c2989c4-688d-4d78-8a71-f02690990d51/content).

3. **Nifty 30-minute ORB** — Nifty 50 spot, 15-minute bars, July 2017–March 2026. Range = 09:15–09:45 IST; first high/low break, maximum one trade/day; stop at opposite range edge; target 2R; otherwise exit 14:30. Optional minimum range 40 points. Reported 2,122 trades, 48.7% wins, PF 1.23, Sharpe 1.16, +91.6%, MDD 11.2%; no costs and no OOS. **Grade B**. **Pine: yes**. Index reconstitution limits classic survivorship concerns, but spot fills are non-executable and later weekday/range filters are post-hoc. With PF only 1.23, Indian taxes, spread and slippage could materially weaken or erase the edge. [Method and code](https://intradaylab.com/blog/nifty-orb-breakout-strategy-backtest).

4. **MNQ ORB falsification** — MNQ, 947 days/72,604 five-minute bars, 2021–2025. Range 09:30–09:55 ET; enter next-bar open after breakout; exit after 1 or 15 bars. Pullback variant enters within five points of the boundary with a 20-point stop. Two-point round-trip friction and expanding-window walk-forward were used. Long bar+1: 447 trades, 51.9%, −0.82 points; long bar+15: 447, 55.5%, +2.82 points but t=1.50 and unstable by year; shorts and pullbacks lost, with pullback win rate 19.3%. PF not reported. **Grade B**: transparent working paper, no released code/data. **Pine: yes**. No survivorship issue, but single-instrument/continuous-contract bias remains. The study's well-supported conclusion is negative: no tested ORB variant met OOS, stability and cost criteria. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04004).
