# orderflow_055

**Topic ID:** orderflow_055  
**Family:** orderflow  
**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Question:** Detecting institutional activity via volume spikes on 1-5m bars: quantified value

---

The strongest direct evidence is negative: a completed 1–5-minute volume spike is generally too late to trade. Volume alone also cannot identify whether institutions, retail traders, market makers, or forced liquidations caused it.

1. Volume-spike momentum — MNQ futures, 5-minute.  
Entry: at a bar close, identify volume in the top decile relative to its rolling 20-bar baseline; buy next-bar open after an up bar, short after a down bar. Exit: next-bar close; no stop. Filter: MNQ RTH 09:30–16:00 ET. Results after two MNQ points round-trip friction ($4/contract): up-spikes N=2,119, 51.6% wins, −1.94 points/trade, t=0.07; down-spikes N=2,409, 50.1%, −2.50 points, t=−0.64. PF unreported. Data: 947 days/72,604 bars, Dec-2021–Aug-2025; expanding walk-forward validation. **Grade B**: detailed preprint, but no shared code/data located. Pine v6: **yes**. Survivorship is immaterial for one futures contract, though continuous-contract construction matters. Large samples and near-zero gross predictive content make overfitting an unlikely explanation for failure. It plainly does not survive retail costs. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04004), [full methodology/results](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/404476167_Structural_Limits_of_OHLCV-Based_Intraday_Signals_in_MNQ_Futures_A_Systematic_Falsification_Study)

2. Five-minute ORB on "Stocks in Play" — US equities, 5-minute.  
Entry: if 09:30–09:35 candle is bullish, place buy-stop at its high; if bearish, sell-stop at its low. Exit: 10% of daily ATR(14) stop or market-on-close; no profit target. Filters: price >$5, prior 14-day ADV ≥1m shares, ATR(14)>$0.50, first-five-minute RVOL ≥1 versus the same interval's prior-14-day mean, and only the daily top-20 RVOL stocks. Results: 2016–2023, point-in-time CRSP universe exceeding 7,000 stocks; 48.4% hit rate, 1,637% total return, 41.6% annualized, Sharpe 2.81, MDD 12%. Trade count and PF were not reported. Commission was $0.0035/share; spread, slippage, borrow constraints and market impact were omitted. No independent OOS. **Grade B**. Pine: **partial**—single-symbol rules are easy, but historical top-20 cross-sectional ranking and delisted-universe reconstruction are not realistically available in Pine. Survivorship was explicitly controlled, but filter discovery on the same sample creates serious overfitting risk. Given breakout-stop execution in news-driven stocks, retail survival is unproven despite the large reported margin. [Study PDF](https://www.wealth-lab.com/api/discussion/download/pdf/8007-ssrn-4729284-1-pdf)

3. High-volume intraday market momentum — SPY, 30-minute signal implemented on 1–15-minute bars.  
Entry: calculate prior-close-to-10:00 return; at 15:30 buy SPY if positive, otherwise short. Exit: 16:00 close; no stop. The first-half-hour volume top tercile, ranked separately within each year, raised predictive R² to 3.1% versus 1.1% in the low-volume tercile. Overall 1993–2013 strategy: 54.37% success, 6.67% annualized return, Sharpe 1.08; post-decimalization bid/ask adjustment reduced return to 4.46%, while 2005+ produced 6.52%. Exact trade count/PF were not reported; approximately one trade per eligible day. OOS R²=1.4%. **Grade A**, peer-reviewed. Pine: **yes**, although exact historical annual-tercile classification is awkward. SPY survivorship risk is low; commissions were excluded and the sample is old, so present-day persistence requires replication. Crucially, the authors found the opening-return effect was barely affected by trade size: "institutional activity" remains an interpretation, not a detected fact. [Journal article](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X18301351), [paper](https://assets.super.so/e46b77e7-ee08-445e-b43f-4ffd88ae0a0e/files/ee7dac49-530b-4950-b5d0-e0b5eee08f2e.pdf)

Bottom line: the defensible 5-minute finding is null. The promising RVOL-ORB result needs independent walk-forward replication with NBBO spreads, slippage, halts and short-borrow costs. Broader research also finds volume shocks predict overnight—not next-session intraday—returns, further weakening the scalping thesis. [Volume-shock study](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5156605)
