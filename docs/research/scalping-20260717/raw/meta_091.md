# Walk-Forward and Out-of-Sample Standards for Intraday Strategy Development

**topic_id:** meta_091  
**family:** meta  
**date:** 2026-07-17  
**research_question:** Walk-forward and out-of-sample standards for intraday strategy development: authoritative guidance.

---

Authoritative literature does **not** prescribe a universal 70/30 split, minimum trade count, or acceptable profit factor. A defensible standard is: freeze the hypothesis; use chronological rolling/expanding walk-forward optimization; concatenate every OOS window; purge/embargo overlapping labels; retain a final untouched forward period; simulate next-bar/bid-ask execution, latency, spread, commissions and slippage; use point-in-time universes including delistings; log every tried specification; and correct multiple testing with White/SPA, Probability of Backtest Overfitting, or Deflated Sharpe. An ordinary holdout is inadequate after repeated experimentation ([PBO](https://www.davidhbailey.com/dhbpapers/backtest-prob.pdf)); DSR adjusts for trial count and non-normal returns ([Bailey–López de Prado](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2460551)); Reality Check evaluates the full rule universe ([Sullivan–Timmermann–White](https://eprints.lse.ac.uk/119144/)). Require cost-stress, parameter-neighbourhood, year/regime, instrument and bootstrap confidence tests—not merely positive OOS P&L.

**1. First-to-last-half-hour momentum — SPY/ten liquid ETFs; 30-minute signal/hold.** Entry: at 15:30 ET, long if return from prior close to 10:00 is positive, otherwise short. Exit: 16:00; no stop. Stronger on high-volatility/volume, recession and macro-news days, but base rule has no filter. 1993–2013 TAQ sample, excluding days with fewer than 500 trades: 6.67% annual return, 6.19% volatility, Sharpe 1.08 versus buy-and-hold 0.29; recursive OOS \(R^2\) 1.4%; reported significant after transaction costs. Win rate/PF/trade count were not reported; roughly 5,000 daily opportunities. **Grade A**, peer-reviewed *Journal of Financial Economics* ([paper](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X18301351)). Pine v6: **yes**, aggregate six 5-minute bars. No survivorship issue, but the evidence ends in 2013 and cost amount is insufficiently disclosed. One highly liquid ETF round trip plausibly survives retail costs, but post-publication validation is mandatory.

**2. Stocks-in-Play 5-minute ORB — US equities.** Filters: price >$5, prior-14-day average volume ≥1m, daily ATR(14)>$0.50; opening five-minute relative volume ≥100%; trade only the top 20. If the 09:30–09:35 candle is bullish, buy-stop at its high; if bearish, sell-stop at its low; doji means no trade. Stop 10% of daily ATR; otherwise exit 16:00. CRSP/IQFeed, >7,000 stocks, 2016–2023: 48.4% hit rate, 1,637% total return, 41.6% IRR, Sharpe 2.81, MDD 12%, versus unfiltered ORB's 41.4% hit rate/Sharpe 0.48. Commission $0.0035/share; trade count and PF omitted. **Grade B**: detailed but no true OOS or public data/code ([paper](https://www.wealth-lab.com/api/discussion/download/pdf/8007-ssrn-4729284-1-pdf)). Pine: **partial**—single-symbol rule yes; contemporaneous top-20 universe ranking no. CRSP includes delistings, reducing survivorship bias. However, timeframe/filter selection is in-sample and spread/slippage are omitted; the reported average edge above 100% relative volume was only 0.08R/trade, so survival in fast "stocks in play" is doubtful.

**3. Cameron ICT scalp — NQ; 1H/15m context, 5m setup, 30-second entry.** Select an old high/low as liquidity target; opposite 5m swing sweep plus direction-confirming close; enter on retrace into a same-direction 30-second fair-value gap. Base exit: ≤12-point stop and 1R target. Higher-RR variant: displacement-swing stop, nearest 5m structural target capped at 5R, minimum 1.5R. Reproducible 2022–2025 sequencing-corrected tick OOS: base 11,391 trades, 48.7% wins, PF 0.81, −3,768.5 points; higher-RR 7,138 trades, 22.9%, PF 0.95, −3,271 points, despite short-sample IS PF 1.68. Cost: only 0.25 point round trip. **Grade A**, public code/data ([GitHub](https://github.com/hindsight-finance/ict-cameron-scalp-model)). Pine 1–15m: **no** for exact reproduction; 30-second/tick ordering is material. No survivorship issue, but continuous-contract construction matters. It already fails under optimistic costs.

**4. MNQ 25-minute ORB — 5m.** Break the 09:30–09:55 range; next-bar-open entry; exit 15 bars later. Long variant: 447 trades, 55.5% wins, +2.82 points/trade after assumed two-point friction, but \(t=1.50\) and yearly means −1.42, +2.43, +7.04: unstable and statistically unproven. **Grade B**, independent unreviewed study ([paper](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04004)). Pine: **yes**. No survivorship issue; specification-search and single-instrument risks remain. Retail-cost survival is unsupported.

Overall: only SPY closing momentum has strong published evidence; the attractive ORB result lacks honest OOS execution evidence, while the best reproducible scalp and broad MNQ tests are negative.
